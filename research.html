Hands-on workshop

From Headline to Hypothesis

Participants receive a real (simplified) news headline about a scientific finding. In small groups, they work backward: what question must the original researchers have asked, what method could answer it, and what evidence would actually prove or disprove the claim? Groups present their reconstructed research design, and a brief group discussion compares it with what the real study actually did.

01 Receive a real science headline (no other details)
02 Reconstruct the likely research question (10 min)
03 Propose a method and the evidence needed to test it
04 Present, then compare with the actual published study

Project ideas to pursue afterward

Mini lit review

Write a 2-page literature review on a question from your own major

Find five credible sources, summarize the current state of knowledge, and identify one clear, unanswered gap.

Replication exercise

Pick a published study and design a smaller replication of it

Sketch a scaled-down version of a real study's method that you could feasibly run on your own campus.

Faculty interview

Interview a professor about how their research question evolved

Document how a real researcher's question changed from their first idea to their published work, and why.
