"Markets are stories told in numbers — learn to read the numbers first."

Why this matters right now Every industry — from tech to retail to healthcare — now runs on market intelligence: who's gaining share, what's driving demand, where the next disruption is coming from. Reading a market clearly, before everyone else agrees on the story, is one of the most transferable skills a student can build, regardless of their eventual major or career.

Beginner-friendly sessions

No finance or economics background needed — these sessions teach how to read a market like an analyst, one concept at a time.

01

Market Sizing in Plain English: TAM, SAM, and SOM

How analysts estimate how big a market really is — and why every business plan needs this number to mean something.

02

Reading a Competitive Landscape

A simple framework for mapping competitors by strength, price, and positioning — without needing an MBA to do it.

03

Financial Statements for Non-Finance People

What a balance sheet, income statement, and cash flow statement are actually telling you, in under an hour.

04

SWOT and PESTEL: Two Frameworks Worth Knowing

The two most-used beginner strategy frameworks, and how to apply them to a real company in under 20 minutes.

05

Spotting Market Disruption Before It's Obvious

The early warning signs analysts look for when an industry is about to be reshaped by a new entrant or technology.

Hands-on workshop

The Market Brief: 40 Minutes to a Recommendation

Teams receive a real (anonymized) industry snapshot — a handful of stats on market size, competitors, and growth trends. Using the SWOT and market-sizing frameworks from this track, each team produces a one-page market brief and a single clear recommendation: enter, avoid, or wait. Teams present their brief and defend their recommendation to a panel of "investors" played by fellow participants.

01 Receive your industry snapshot and competitor data
02 Build a quick SWOT and rough TAM/SAM/SOM estimate
03 Write a one-page brief with a clear recommendation
04 Present and defend your recommendation to the panel
