Cybersecurity & Threat Intelligence

The Watch
Floor

"Think like the attacker for an afternoon so you can defend like an analyst for a career."

Why this matters right now Ransomware, insider threats, and infrastructure attacks have moved from rare headlines to routine news. Every organization — banks, hospitals, government ministries, small startups — now needs people who can reason clearly about vulnerabilities, classify attacks correctly, and respond fast. This track builds that reasoning from the ground up, using real, documented incidents.

Session lineup

Beginner-friendly sessions

No security background assumed. Sessions build a shared analytical vocabulary using real, documented cases.

01

The CIA Triad: Confidentiality, Integrity, Availability

The foundational framework analysts use to classify what any attack actually damages — and why all three matter differently.

02

Passive vs. Active Attacks: Spotting the Difference

How analysts distinguish silent surveillance-style attacks from active, disruptive ones — and why the distinction changes the response.

03

Anatomy of an Insider Threat

Using a real bank insider-threat case, this session walks through how trusted access becomes the easiest attack vector.

04

DDoS, Explained With a Real Government Attack

A walkthrough of a real-world distributed denial-of-service incident — what made it effective, and what stopped it.

05

Social Engineering & Brute Force: The Human Vulnerability

Why the weakest point in most systems is a person, not a server — and how attackers exploit trust before they exploit code.

Hands-on workshop

Tabletop Incident Response: 30 Minutes to Contain a Breach

Teams are handed a realistic, evolving incident briefing — vague at first, like a real alert. Every five minutes, a new piece of evidence arrives. Using the three-question framework from this track (vulnerable points and countermeasures, passive vs. active classification, and CIA Triad impact), teams must classify the attack, identify the entry point, and propose a containment plan before time runs out.

01 Receive the initial incident alert (intentionally vague)
02 Classify it: passive or active, and which CIA pillar is at risk
03 Respond to new evidence as it's revealed every 5 minutes
04 Present your containment plan and reasoning to the room
