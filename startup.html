Startup & Entrepreneurship

The Founder's
Garage

"From a napkin idea to a pitch you'd actually fund."

Why this matters right now Campus founders are launching with smaller teams, leaner budgets, and AI-assisted tooling than any generation before them — the barrier to building a real product in a weekend has nearly disappeared. What hasn't gotten easier is validating an idea, telling its story, and convincing the first ten people to care. Those are learnable, teachable skills, and this track teaches them.

Session lineup

Beginner-friendly sessions

No business background required. Each session stands alone, but they build naturally toward the closing pitch workshop.

01

What Actually Makes an Idea "Startup-Worthy"

The difference between a good idea and a fundable one: problem size, urgency, and who's desperate enough to pay for a fix.

02

Talking to Strangers: A Beginner's Guide to Validation

How to test an idea before writing a line of code — customer interviews, landing-page tests, and reading "no" correctly.

03

The Lean Canvas in 30 Minutes

One page, nine boxes: a fast way to sketch a business model without a finance degree.

04

Storytelling for Founders

Why investors fund narratives, not features — structuring a pitch so the problem lands before the solution does.

05

Funding 101: Bootstrapping, Friends & Family, and What VCs Actually Want

A plain-language map of how startups get money, and which path fits a first-time student founder.

Hands-on workshop

The 5-Minute Pitch Sprint

Teams of three to four get a randomly assigned, slightly absurd everyday problem (e.g. "people forget where they parked"). In 35 minutes, they sketch a lean canvas, write one sentence of customer validation logic, and build a 3-slide pitch deck. Every team pitches live for 90 seconds, followed by two minutes of rapid-fire audience questions — modeled on a real investor Q&A.

01 Form a team and receive your problem card (10 min)
02 Fill out a one-page lean canvas together (10 min)
03 Build a 3-slide pitch: problem, solution, ask (15 min)
04 Pitch live and field two minutes of audience Q&A
